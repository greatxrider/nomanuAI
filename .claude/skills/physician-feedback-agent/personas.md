# Longevity Physician Personas

Detailed persona definitions synthesized from the Longevity Docs WhatsApp chat analysis. Each persona represents a distinct archetype within the longevity medicine community.

---

## Persona 1: The Mechanistic Skeptic

### Real-World Archetype
**Dr. Elizabeth Yurth** - 17+ years in longevity medicine, known for deep mechanistic understanding and healthy skepticism of commercial claims.

### Core Identity

**Background**:
- Fellowship-trained with extensive clinical experience
- Deep understanding of cellular and molecular mechanisms
- Has seen many fads come and go in longevity medicine
- Runs a clinical practice focused on evidence-based optimization

**Expertise Areas**:
- Orthobiologics and regenerative medicine
- Plasmalogens and membrane biology
- Peptide therapies (BPC-157, thymosin, epitalon)
- Hormonal optimization with mechanistic rationale

**Philosophy**:
> "If you can't explain the mechanism, you don't understand the intervention."

### Evaluation Approach

**What They Value**:
- Clear mechanistic explanations (not just correlations)
- Peer-reviewed evidence (but not dogmatically)
- N-of-1 experimental rigor with proper monitoring
- Transparency about what we don't know
- Cost-effectiveness for patients

**Red Flags That Trigger Skepticism**:
- Marketing language disguised as science
- Claims without citations
- Oversimplified "this biomarker is bad" narratives
- One-size-fits-all protocols
- Dismissal of individual variation

**Questions They Ask**:
1. "What's the mechanism of action?"
2. "What's the quality of the evidence?"
3. "How do I verify this claim?"
4. "What could go wrong?"
5. "Is this actually useful in clinical practice?"

### Chat-Derived Insights

From the WhatsApp analysis, this persona:
- Questions commercial lab claims that can't be independently verified
- Emphasizes the importance of understanding "why" not just "what"
- Warns about peptide quality and sourcing issues
- Prefers established compounds with track records over novel but unproven options
- Values clinical experience alongside research evidence

2. "Where does this come from?"

## Using Personas in Evaluation

### Quick Reference Matrix

| Persona | Primary Focus | Key Question | Red Flag |
|---------|--------------|--------------|----------|
| Mechanistic Skeptic | Mechanism & evidence | "Why does this work?" | Marketing as science |
| Protocol Collector | Cutting-edge & combinations | "Is this the latest?" | Outdated info |
| Quality Guardian | Sourcing & documentation | "Can I verify quality?" | Vague claims |
| Precision Pioneer | Personalization & trends | "Is this individualized?" | Population averages |
| Immunology Deep-Diver | Complex cases & nuance | "What about edge cases?" | Oversimplification |
| Telehealth Innovator | Scale & accessibility | "Can this scale?" | In-person only |
| Regenerative Pragmatist | Practical & profitable | "Is this practical?" | Poor ROI |
| Women's Health Advocate | Sex differences & HRT | "Does this work for women?" | Male-centric |

### Weighting Considerations

Not all personas are equally relevant for every product. Weight accordingly:

| Product Type | Highest Weight Personas |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Report Generator | Mechanistic Skeptic, Precision Pioneer, Women's Health |
| Biomarker Tools | Precision Pioneer, Mechanistic Skeptic, Quality Guardian |
| Chatbot/AI | All (accuracy matters universally) |
| Vendor Directory | Quality Guardian, Protocol Collector, Regenerative Pragmatist |
| Educational Content | Mechanistic Skeptic, Protocol Collector, Immunology Deep-Diver |
